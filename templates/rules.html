Hexachromix is a color mixing strategy game. The goal is to connect opposite sides of the board with your color.

Terminology

  • RGB  –  Red, Green, Blue

    aka the "additive/light primaries" or "secondary subtractive colors" (more info)

  • CMY  –  Cyan, Magenta, Yellow

    aka the "subtractive/pigment primaries" or "secondary additive colors" (more info)

  • Color system  –  one of the above sets of three colors

Mixology

The diagrams below demonstrate how color mixing works for the RGB and CMY color systems. Notice how mixing two colors from the same system produces a color in the other system. This is the key game mechanic of Hexachromix.

Gameplay -ology?

Hexachromix can be played with 2-6 players. All six colors must be controlled by someone. A player may control multiple colors, as long as those colors are on the same team (see Variants for more information).

Red goes first and play continues clockwise (RYGCBM order). You may not skip a turn. If there are no moves available on your turn, the game ends in a draw.

There are three kinds of legal moves:

  • Claim an empty space
  • Share a space with only one of the other colors from the same color system

    This applies whether or not the other color is on your team.

  • Mix a space containing both of the colors from the other system which, when mixed, produce your color

    This applies whether or not the colors are on your team.

Variants

All variants use the same rules and turn order (RYGCBM). However, you may find that these variants call for varied strategies and tactics.

There are many ways to divide the six colors into teams. A player may control any number of colors on the same team. As mentioned in the Gameplay section, all colors must be controlled by someone.

Winning

The first player who connects their side of the board to the opposite side, via adjacent spaces containing their color, is the winner. The color that makes such a connection wins the game for the whole team.
